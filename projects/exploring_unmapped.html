Exploring Umapped Terrain with Multi-Robot System

Description:

Exploring unmapped terrain is difficult because of the uncertainity of the robots as well as the unknown dangers in the terrain. Hence, a group of robots is effective to explore the terrain than just one robot. In this project, the exploration of the terrain is done using an optimized random algorithm. Once one of the robots identifies the goal, it send a signal to the other robots indicating its position so that the others can reach the goal.

Robot Design and Architecture:

Four Lego EV3 differential-drive robots are used to explore the terrain. To simulate GPS, an overhead camera is used. The detection of goal is also simulated by sending commands to the robots when the camera detects that the robot has reached the goal.
ROS is used to communicate the commands to the robots. OpenCV is used for image processing.

Algorithm:

The robots are dispersed in an optimized way. Once one of the robot reached the goal, the central master plans the path for other robots using A-star algorithm and splines are used to generate trajectories for the other robots.
